About Me

Hello! I am Dylan Diaz and I study computer science. I am from Boiling Springs, South Carolina. When I graduate and gain experience, I would love to eventually work for NASA! I want to help code rovers and other technology that will expand humanity's knowledge of the universe.

Why I chose USC

I chose USC because it was only about an hour and a half from home. USC gave me more money than Clemson and other schools I was considering. I also heard that USC was academically a good school.

Transition

Transitioning to USC was pretty tough at first. It was strange being away from home. It was a completely new experience. Some of my first thoughts were of self-doubt. I struggled with the work load at first. It was so different from high school.Especially with my Algorithmic Design class.

However, the transition was not terrible. I have many favorite memories here. I remember meeting my roommates for the first time. I remember staying up really late one night playing videogames with them. I remember getting my first math test back and it was actually a decent grade.

Academic Transition

The difference between classes from high school and college made me struggle at first. I procrastinated a lot, I did not study for awhile, and it caused me a lot of stress. Combine that with the immense workload of college, and I was pretty stressed out at first. To make the transition, I just had to stop procrastinating and start studying. As soon as I did that, I noticed a huge change in how stressed I was. The TI's in my classes also helped a lot, as I was able to ask them questions. Another USC resource I used was the Career Center. One assignment for a class was to make a resume, and the Career Center reviewed it and gave me feedback before I turned it in.

Favorite Part of Being at USC

My favorite part of being a student at USC has been all the people I have met. I have become pretty close with my roommates.

Next Semester

I have learned a lot about myself this semester. I have learned that I can hangle the workload and stress of college. I have learned that I can balance getting good grades and having a social life. With this information, I will go into next semester more confident than I was going into this semester. I will also try to step out of my comfort zone more next semester, now that I have handled the transition of moving to college. If I could give one piece of advice to incoming freshmen, it would be to stay on top of assignments and not procrastinate.
